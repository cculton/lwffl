II. Rosters

Roster settings closely reflect ESPN standard leagues. Each roster consists of: QB (1), RB (2), WR (2), WR/TE (1), FLEX (1), D/ST (1), K (1), BENCH (6) and IR (2).

RBs, WRs and TEs are eligible to be played in the FLEX position.

Players who are declared “out” are eligible to be placed in the IR slot. If a player placed in the IR slot no longer carries an injury designation, the team’s roster will be locked from making transactions until either 1) roster modifications allow the player in the IR slot is moved to the active roster or 2) another IR-eligible player is exchanged with the healthy player in the IR slot.

This league observes the ESPN undroppables list to reduce the risk of collusion or other strategies that threaten to erode the competitive balance of the league.

IV. Draft Procedure

This league uses an auction-style (salary cap) draft with a $200 budget, conducted at a time determined to be the best for the majority of managers. It should be held no earlier than the conclusion of the second week of the NFL preseason, and no later than 24 hours before the first NFL regular season game, except under unusual circumstances. managers unable to draft at a tentatively decided time should notify one of the LMs of the conflict as soon as possible. The draft can be moved on draft day to adjust for unforeseen circumstances if there is unanimous league approval.

V. Regular Season Schedule

The regular season consists of 14 one-week matchups. Each team faces their division opponents twice per season (6 weeks) and non-division opponents once per season (8 weeks). The regular season has two, three-week divisional matchup periods, one mid-season and one to conclude the regular season in weeks 12-14. There is not a tiebreaker for regular season matchups.

Standings in each division are determined by winning percentage. If two teams have the same winning percentage, “points for” is used as the tiebreaker.

VI. Free Agency

This league uses a system ESPN refers to as “Free Agent Auction Bidding,” or FAAB, for acquiring players via free agency. Each team starts the season with the same budget of $100 that can be spent on free agents. Submitted bids process early each Wednesday morning. There is no minimum bid for free agents (i.e., managers may bid $0). If two or more managers bid the same amount for a player, ties are broken by reverse order of standings.

After bids are processed each week, all unclaimed players become free agents and can be freely added on a first come, first serve basis. All free agents will lock again once their game kicks off for that week. Any players who are dropped following waivers processing on Wednesday morning will go on waivers for a period of 1 day and be subject to the FAAB rules listed above.

FAAB dollars are not tradeable and they do not rollover from season to season.

The FAAB budgets of teams who do not qualify for the playoffs are set to $0 upon the conclusion of the regular season. Teams eliminated during the playoffs will also have their FAAB budgets set to $0 at the conclusion of the week they are eliminated. This allows eliminated teams to make acquisitions without interfering with those in contention.

VII. Trades

Trades are processed automatically after acceptance. There is no trade review period.

If there is ever an objection to an accepted trade, a review process can be initiated by sending a message to the Discord league chat or individually to either of the League Managers. The League Managers would then jointly review the trade to determine if it seems reasonable. The only situation where the League Managers would determine a veto is necessary is if the trade threatens to erode the integrity and/or competitive balance of the league (i.e., collusion).

Should a review be requested on a trade involving one of the League Managers, the other League Manager will individually determine if the trade is reasonable or if a veto is required. If a trade review is requested on a trade between the two League Managers, it will be up to the rest of the league to determine if the trade is reasonable or if a veto is required.

The league’s trade deadline is the Wednesday prior to Week 11 at noon EST.

VIII. Postseason

Playoff Seeding & Tiebreakers

Six of the 12 teams in the league advance to the playoffs. The division winners are reserved the No. 1-3 seeds in the playoffs, with seeding determined by winning percentage. The No. 4 seed is the “record wildcard” and is filled by the remaining team with the best winning percentage regardless of division. The No. 5-6 seeds are the “points wildcards” and are filled by the remaining two teams with the most “points for,” regardless of division.

In the event that one or more teams have the same winning percentage, tiebreakers are executed in the following order as necessary:

  1. Most total “points for” in regular season
  2. Head-to-head record in regular season
  3. Most total “points against” in regular season
  4. Coin flip

Playoff Schedule

The playoffs span three weeks, running from Week 15 in the NFL season through Week 17. Each playoff matchup lasts one week, and the top two seeds receive a bye week in the first round. In the event of a tie, the tiebreakers in the previous subsection will be applied.

Eliminated playoff teams during the semi-finals round will participate in a “3rd place game” that will have prize money implications, as outlined below in section IX on payout structure. Each eliminated playoff team will have its FAAB budget set to $0 to allow acquisitions that will not interfere with teams still in the playoffs.

Playoff Matchups

During the first two rounds of the playoffs, the top active seed will choose their opponent. Therefore in the First Round, the No. 3 seed will have its choice between the 4, 5, or 6 seeds. In the Semifinals, the No. 1 seed will have its choice between the remaining three playoff teams. If no choice is submitted, the highest active seed will be assigned the lowest active seed.

Consolation Bracket

Each manager in the consolation bracket will have its FAAB budget set to $0 to allow acquisitions that won’t interfere with teams competing in the playoffs.

The consolation bracket follows the same format as the playoff bracket. There is no prize or benefit earned for winning the consolation bracket.

IX. Buy-In Process & Payout Structure

Buy-In

This league has a $75 buy-in, which is collected using Venmo. The buy-in must be paid by Aug. 31 at 11:59 p.m EDT each season.

A late payment may be subject to a fee. A late payment will also automatically result in a 10-point deduction from the late-paying team’s Week 1 matchup. A 10-point deduction will be indefinitely assessed for each subsequent matchup if the buy-in is still not received. If a payment is received after kickoff of the first game of a matchup (typically Thursday night), a 10-point deduction will still be assessed for that week.

Any manager that does not pay before Oct. 1 will not be eligible to collect prize money. If an ineligible team wins prize money, the amount will be put in the following year’s pool or distributed to the other managers in equal amounts, whichever the co-LMs decide.

If one or more teams do not pay, the lower amount of prize money will be divided by percentage based on a $900 pool.

Payout

The following accomplishments merit a portion of the $900 of available prize money each season:

League Champion $450
2nd Place $175
3rd Place $100
Regular Season Points Leader $100
4th Place $75

Winnings will be distributed within two weeks of the fantasy season’s conclusion through Venmo.

X. Disputes, Amendment Process & League Manager Powers

Disputes

If there is ever a dispute about a matter not addressed in this constitution, the co-LMs will allow time for a league-wide discussion about possible resolutions. This discussion period will vary depending on the matter’s time-sensitivity.

After the discussion period, a majority vote (seven of 12 managers) will determine the outcome. If there are abstentions, the amount of votes needed for a majority will shift accordingly. If a vote results in a tie, the co-LMs will have final say. New rules pertaining to the outcome of a dispute can be added to the constitution as an amendment if the co-LMs deem it necessary..

Amendments

There is a week-long window each year prior to each season designated for amendment proposals, which can pertain to any of the league’s rules or settings. The amendment period will begin no later than Aug. 14 each year. Amendment proposals should be presented in the form of a poll in the league’s Discord, under the amendment polling channel.

Each proposal should be clear and thorough. Polls should include only two options: “for” and “against.”

All 12 managers will have one vote on each amendment proposal, and proposals will require at least three (3) votes of approval in addition to zero (0) dissenting votes to pass. This means that one dissenting vote is enough to nullify a proposal. However, if a proposal receives nine (9) votes of approval or more, it will pass even with dissenting votes.

Each poll will be open for three days, or 72 hours, from the time it is posted. Once a proposal expires, one of the co-LMs will post an official outcome.

Each manager is only allowed to have one proposal active at a time. If a proposal fails, the author is encouraged to engage in constructive discussion with other league members. Failed proposals can be reworked and eventually re-proposed if one so chooses.

Amendments can be proposed outside of this period, but such proposals require unanimous approval.

LM Powers

Among other powers outlined in this document, the co-LMs reserve the power to:

  • Remove people from and invite people to the league
  • Levy punishments
  • Edit without notice items in this document deemed to be immaterial
